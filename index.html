I am a postdoctoral fellow at University of Rochester under the supervision of Professors Ashkan Ertefaie and Robert Strawderman. Here, I work to develop causal inference methods to study the impact of air pollution and cardiovascular events.

I obtained my Ph.D. at University of Waterloo with the thesis Design and Analysis of Experimens on Networks. My advisors are Professor Stefan Steiner and Professor Nathaniel Stevens. During my Ph.D., I also worked as a statistical consultant at the Statistical Consulting and Survey Research Unit (SCSRU), where I consulted 80+ scientific projects.

I completed my undergraduate studies with Double Majors in Mathematics and Business at Korean Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST). I love music, art and languagues.
